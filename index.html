EasyReach360 (Patent Pending)

Designed and built the EasyReach360, an adaptive desk with a rotating center, allowing individuals with limited mobility or disabilities to achieve independence by making objects within reach. The desk is user-friendly and can be operated using adaptive switches tailored to different disabilities.

TKT Glove

Designed and built the Thermal Kinetic Therapeutic Glove. An adaptive glove that vibrates (to circulate blood flow) and heats up (to keep his hands warm) to mitigate the effects of Raynaud's disease.

QR Pals

Nassau BOCES Rosemary Kennedy School houses a classroom in my school. While visiting, my friend and I observed that numerous students relied on task analysis cards. Inspired by this, we created a website called www.qrpals.com, a website that seamlessly integrates task analysis cards with QR codes containing audio instructions. Upon a returning visit, I observed that QR Pal technology was prominently displayed throughout the room, aiding the students in various tasks like handwashing. Recently, I discovered that the students were also employing the QR codes at home, thereby enhancing their independence.

Beautifying UCPN

While visiting the Cerebral Palsy Association of Nassau County (CP Nassau), I noticed the deteriorating ornamental exterior wall art. Having been able to envision the beauty that it once was, I became inspired to bring it back to its former glory. I recruited and led a team to assist with the project. Designing, building, painting, weatherproofing, and installing took a little over a month. The look on the students' faces along with their teachers and administrators was confirmation that the project was a success.

Coloring Poster

During the school year, I hung up a coloring poster with inspirational messages, colored pencils, and a sign explaining the science behind how coloring reduces anxiety. Students, teachers, and administrators came together to create a piece of art. After seeing how beautiful it turned out, I auctioned off the poster for $1000. I spent all the funds on toys, games, and activities and hand-delivered them to improve the social emotional well-being of the children at Cohen Children’s Medical Center. I continue to hang coloring posters in my school.

Fishing Club

As an avid fisherman, it troubles me to witness the prevalence of unsafe fishing practices and the disregard for marine etiquette. Therefore, I created my high school's first Fishing Club with over seventy members and arranged a partnership with the NYS Department of Environmental Conservation, I FISH NY. I organize fishing trips, taught proper stewardship, and led dogfish dissections. Members expressed concerns about a lack of supplies, so I secured donations through bait and tackle shops and designed and created 3D-printed eco-friendly lures.

Hooks For Heroes

I volunteer and organize events for Hooks For Heroes, a non-profit that gives back to American Veterans and First Responders by providing them with free of cost fishing trips to help cope with the physical and mental injuries during their service. The Events include: a shark tournament, a Duck Race to raise money for the local Fire Department, and selling merchandise to raise money for First Responders.
